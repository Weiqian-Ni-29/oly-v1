Pre-printing drying is a critical pretreatment step in the production of membrane panels, primarily aimed at removing moisture, oil contaminants, or processing residues from the substrate surface. This ensures optimal adhesion and pattern precision in subsequent printing processes. By precisely controlling drying temperature and duration, the surface tension of the substrate can be effectively enhanced, preventing issues such as ink bleeding, poor adhesion, or bubble formation during printing.

6

Ink Color Mixing Process

The ink color matching process for membrane panels mainly includes: After receiving customer color samples, professional equipment is used to collect standard color data; appropriate inks are selected and formulated based on substrate characteristics; the formula is optimized through proofing verification and color difference detection; after finalizing the formula, ink viscosity is adjusted and standard processes are established; during production, strict control is implemented for first-piece inspection and sampling inspection while monitoring environmental conditions; finally, a color database is created to store color matching records, with regular maintenance of testing equipment to ensure color accuracy and batch stability.

7

Membrane Panel Printing Process

We employ internationally advanced silver paste printing technology to produce high-precision conductive circuits on various flexible film substrates. This mature process, incorporating professional surface treatment, precision printing control, and strict quality management, ensures excellent conductivity and long-term reliability. With stable process performance and extensive application experience, we have provided premium membrane switches, flexible circuits, and other product solutions to numerous electronic device manufacturers. From product design to mass production, we offer professional technical support and customized services to meet diverse application requirements across different scenarios.

8

Membrane Panel Backlighting Process

The backlighting process for membrane panels is a critical manufacturing step that integrates LED or other light-emitting components into the panel structure. This process begins with precisely positioning LED chips or backlight modules at designated locations on the panel using high-precision placement equipment. Electrical connections between the light-emitting components and conductive circuits are then established through reliable methods such as conductive silver adhesive bonding or thermal compression welding.

Visual Inspection

In the standardized quality inspection area, operators must wear cleanroom gloves and inspect the products under professional testing light sources. They shall hold the products in a standardized manner and conduct detailed visual examinations from multiple angles. Any products with noticeable scratches, visible stains or foreign matter, detectable dents/deformation, or uneven coatings shall be immediately classified as non-conforming items.

12

Electrical Testing

During electrical testing, we conduct comprehensive performance evaluations using professional equipment to verify all parameters meet design specifications. Key tests include continuity checks, insulation resistance measurements, and voltage withstand tests, accurately identifying issues such as short circuits, open circuits, or signal abnormalities. All non-conforming products are automatically flagged and isolated, ensuring the reliability and safety of outgoing products.
